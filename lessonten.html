Weather and Climate of Myanmar


The climate pattern of Myanmar can be divided into an annual cycle of three seasons,namely,summer,monsoon and winter.In summer,those areas in the the central Myanmar have had temperatures much higher than the remaining parts of the country.During the rainy season,those costal areas along the Rakhine coast,the Ayeyawaddy delta and the Taninthayi coast get very heavy rainfalls.In winter,those areas in the northernmost reaches of Myanmar and,those in the Shan plateau which is situated at an average altitude of 3000 to over 4000 feet above sea level are very cold.As Myanmar is situated in the tropical zone of the world the country enjoys,basically,some moderate climatic conditions and fine weather during its annual three-season cycle.It,therefore,relies mainly on agriculture and livestock breeding and fisheries.
