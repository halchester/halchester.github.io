The availability of sunlight


The availability of sunlight may vary from a region to a region of the world. This is because of the different angles of sunlight hitting the earth â€“ direct rays and slanted rays- and a different length of time the earth is hit by sunlight. Consequently, regions closer to the Equator is hot as hit by direct sun rays compared to the polar regions of Earth that are hit by slanted rays are cold. Thus, low latitude zones are referred as the tropical zone while high latitude zones are generally called the temperate zone. In other words, temperature is high when daylight lasts long and when daylight lasts short temperature is low.
